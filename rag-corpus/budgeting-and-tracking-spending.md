# Budgeting and Tracking Your Spending

## What a Budget Does

A budget is a plan for how you will use the money that comes in each month. Building
one helps you see whether you have enough to cover your bills, find money to save,
and avoid running short before your next paycheck. The CFPB's approach breaks
budgeting into a few simple, repeatable steps.

## Step 1: Identify Your Income

Add up all the money that comes in. Income may include more than a single paycheck —
self-employment, a second job, child support, or government benefits all count. An
income tracker helps you record every source so you know exactly how much you have
to work with.

## Step 2: Track Where Your Money Goes

Log your spending so you get a realistic picture of where your money actually goes on
average. A spending tracker lets you record purchases and sort them into categories
such as:

- Housing (rent or mortgage)
- Utilities
- Groceries
- Eating out
- Transportation
- Entertainment

A practical method: get a receipt each time you spend, and drop it in an envelope or
container. If you don't get a receipt, jot down the amount and what you bought. Once
people track their spending, many find money they can redirect toward savings or use
to balance their budget.

## Step 3: Keep a Bill Calendar

Mark when each of your bills is due on a calendar. This helps you avoid missed or
late payments and shows you the weeks when money will be tight, so you can plan your
spending carefully around those dates.

## Step 4: Pull It Together in a Budget

Once you know your income, your spending by category, and when your bills are due, a
budget worksheet brings everything into one place. Compare your total income against
your total spending. If you are spending more than you bring in, look at your
tracked categories to find where you can cut back. If you have money left over,
direct it toward savings or paying down debt.

## Sticking With It

- Review your budget regularly and update it when your income or expenses change.
- Build in a small amount for savings and treat it as a fixed bill.
- Use your tracked categories to spot patterns — small recurring purchases often add
  up to more than people expect.
